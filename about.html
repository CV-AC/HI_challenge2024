Participate HI challenge

Participating form

We require participants submite their research ideas in the submission format that adheres to the submission format from Research Council of Finland (a.k.a. Academy of Finland) Template for Academy Project. This format is designed to ensure a comprehensive presentation of the research objectives, methodologies, and anticipated outcomes.

We welcome full long proposal submissions (between 5 and 8 pages, excluding references or supplementary materials).

At the day of the workshop on Arctic AI day 2024, November, oral presentations will be conducted by authors who are attending in-person.

The Winners will be announced on Arctic AI day 2024, November.

All proposal should be submitted using this CMT website.

Evaluation Criteria

1. Relevance and Alignment: How well the proposal aligns with the goals of the hybrid intelligence challenge. This includes the relevance of the topic and its potential to contribute to the field.

2. Innovativeness: The novelty of the research approach and its contribution to advancing knowledge in hybrid intelligence. This looks at whether the proposal introduces new methods, theories, or technologies.

3. Interdisciplinary Approach: The effectiveness and coherence of integrating different disciplinary perspectives and methodologies within the research team.

4. Methodological Rigor: The clarity and appropriateness of the proposed methodology, including data collection, analysis techniques, and the feasibility of the research design.

5. Team Competence: The expertise and experience of the research team in relation to the proposed project. This includes the ability to successfully execute the proposed research.

6. Impact Potential: The expected outcomes and their significance in terms of scientific, technological, educational, or societal impact.

7. Sustainability and Ethics: Consideration of long-term impacts, sustainability, and ethical issues related to the research from the initial idea to its execution and potential implementation.

Ethical Considerations

All submission must provide a separate section for Ethical considerations (as included in the Template) in which the team identifies all the relevant research ethical issues they need to address in the frameworks of research integrity and ethics. Being aware of ethical guidelines and regulations is essential. Furthermore, the team should motivate their proposal also in ethical terms (e.g., why is the proposal worth carrying out, what kind of values are intrinsic or instrumental in it, who and what are the relevant stakeholders, what kind of issues follow from the success of the proposal and what kind of undesirable and harmful consequences avoided).
